# Privacy Policy for LiftXP

**Effective date: July 23, 2026**

LiftXP: Ranked Gym Workouts (“LiftXP,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) is a workout, strength, body-weight, nutrition, and fitness-progress tracking application.

This Privacy Policy explains what information LiftXP handles, how it is used, when it is shared with service providers, and what choices you have.

## 1. Information Stored on Your Device

LiftXP does not currently require you to create an account.

Most information you enter into LiftXP is stored locally on your device. This may include:

- Your name and onboarding selections
- Age, gender, height, weight, activity level, and fitness goals
- Workout routines
- Exercise selections, sets, repetitions, and weights
- Workout history and session notes
- Personal records and estimated one-repetition maximums
- Exercise and muscle-group ranks
- Body-weight entries
- Nutrition and macronutrient entries
- Nutrition targets and preferences
- Progress graphs
- Saved goals
- Active-workout recovery information
- App preferences
- Your saved AI-permission choice

This information is not uploaded to a LiftXP account or LiftXP-operated cloud database.

Deleting LiftXP normally removes locally stored app data. Your device or operating system may retain or restore information through device backups outside LiftXP’s control.

LiftXP does not currently provide account-based cloud backup or synchronization.

## 2. Optional AI Features

LiftXP includes optional AI-powered features for:

- Generating workouts from selected preferences
- Editing AI-generated workouts
- Estimating meals from written descriptions
- Estimating meals from photos
- Estimating meals from voice recordings
- Producing BodyScan visual fitness scores from physique photos

Before LiftXP sends content to an AI provider for the first time, the app displays a disclosure and asks for permission.

If permission is granted, that choice is saved locally on the device and applies to LiftXP’s AI features. Permission can be withdrawn at any time through LiftXP’s Settings. Withdrawing permission prevents new AI requests but does not undo processing that occurred while permission was enabled.

Depending on the feature, information sent for AI processing may include:

- A meal description
- A full meal photo
- A meal-related note
- A voice recording describing a meal
- Answers provided to AI follow-up questions
- A selected workout objective
- Selected muscle groups or body-area focus
- Session duration and available equipment
- Exercises in an AI-generated workout
- Instructions entered to change a workout
- A full physique photo selected for BodyScan
- People, objects, backgrounds, or identifying details visible in a submitted photo
- Instructions added by LiftXP that are necessary to generate the requested result

For workout generation, LiftXP does not intentionally include your name or unrelated saved profile answers unless the feature clearly tells you otherwise.

AI requests are sent from your device to OpenRouter, which routes them to Google’s Gemini models. OpenRouter and the selected model provider may also receive standard technical information such as an IP address, request metadata, token counts, timing information, safety-filter information, and similar operational data.

LiftXP configures its OpenRouter requests to require zero-data-retention-compatible routing. This is intended to prevent submitted prompts, photos, recordings, and responses from being retained for ordinary provider logging or model training. OpenRouter may retain non-content request metadata. OpenRouter or its model providers may also process limited information when necessary for security, abuse prevention, billing, service operation, or legal compliance.

LiftXP does not use AI-submitted content for advertising.

AI features are optional. Manual workout and nutrition features remain available without enabling AI.

Do not submit medical records, financial information, passwords, confidential information, or other information you do not want processed by an external AI service.

## 3. Meal and Physique Photos

When you take or select a photo for an AI feature, LiftXP processes the specific image you chose.

For meal analysis, the image may be sent together with an optional note and answers to follow-up questions.

For BodyScan, LiftXP shows the selected physique photo for review before analysis. The entire selected image may be sent, including any person, object, background, or identifying detail visible in it.

Photos prepared for AI processing are held in app memory or temporary storage as needed to complete the request. LiftXP does not upload them to a LiftXP-operated photo database.

LiftXP does not delete or modify the original image in your photo library.

If you save a BodyScan result, LiftXP creates a rendered BodyScan card and saves it to your photo library at your direction. A temporary rendering may remain in app or operating-system temporary storage until it is cleared by the app, the operating system, storage management, or deletion of the app.

If you share a BodyScan card, the card is provided to the application or service you choose through the device’s sharing interface. Information shared through another application is handled according to that application’s privacy practices.

## 4. Voice Input

When you use voice-based meal logging, LiftXP records the audio needed to process your request.

The recording is sent for AI processing only after AI permission has been granted.

Voice recordings may remain temporarily in the app’s cache until cleared by LiftXP, the operating system, device-storage management, or deletion of the app.

LiftXP does not use voice recordings for advertising or user profiling.

You can avoid voice processing by typing the meal manually.

## 5. Analytics and Product Usage

LiftXP uses PostHog to understand app usage, improve onboarding and product flows, measure feature engagement, troubleshoot problems, and understand whether features are working as intended.

Analytics information may include:

- An anonymous app or installation identifier
- IP address
- Approximate location derived from an IP address
- Device type and model
- Operating-system name and version
- App version
- Language, locale, region, and similar technical information
- App lifecycle events
- Screen names and navigation information
- Route or screen parameters
- Touch-interaction metadata and component identifiers
- Onboarding steps viewed, completed, skipped, or revisited
- Time spent on onboarding steps
- Workout-session start and completion events
- Routine titles
- Exercise counts
- Workout duration
- Energy-level selections
- Experience points associated with a workout
- Routine creation or editing events
- Meal logging and deletion events
- Estimated calories, protein, ingredient count, and meal-input method
- AI-workout objectives, focus selections, duration, routine title, and exercise count
- Feature-flag and related operational events

Some analytics fields, such as a routine title or screen parameter, may contain information entered or selected by the user. LiftXP does not intentionally send meal photos, physique photos, voice recordings, full meal descriptions, or complete workout histories to PostHog.

PostHog touch autocapture is enabled for interaction analytics. LiftXP limits custom touch properties to configured component identifiers such as `testID`. PostHog session replay is not enabled. LiftXP records screen views through its own analytics integration.

Analytics are not used for third-party advertising or cross-app advertising tracking.

## 6. Subscriptions, Paywalls, and Payments

LiftXP may offer auto-renewable subscriptions, introductory offers, or free trials.

Payments, billing, eligibility, renewals, cancellations, and refunds are handled by Apple through the App Store. LiftXP does not receive or store your full payment-card information.

LiftXP uses Superwall to display paywalls, determine subscription access, and measure subscription and paywall performance.

Information processed by Superwall may include:

- An app-specific or vendor device identifier
- App version
- Operating-system version
- Device model
- Device language, locale, region, and time zone
- App installation timing
- Paywall views and interactions
- Purchase history
- Subscription status
- Whether the app is running in a sandbox or production environment
- Other technical information needed to provide and analyze paywalls

LiftXP does not intentionally provide your name, email address, workout history, meal content, meal photos, physique photos, or voice recordings to Superwall.

You can manage or cancel an App Store subscription through:

**Settings → Your Name → Subscriptions → LiftXP**

Deleting LiftXP does not automatically cancel an active subscription.

## 7. Notifications

LiftXP does not currently operate an account-based marketing notification system. Locally scheduled notification information may remain on your device until the notification is delivered, cancelled, or cleared.

## 8. Exercise Rankings and Fitness Estimates

LiftXP calculates exercise ranks, muscle-group rankings, performance percentiles, estimated one-repetition maximums, nutrition targets, and other fitness estimates.

These calculations may use information such as:

- Exercise type
- Weight lifted
- Repetitions completed
- Estimated one-repetition maximum
- Body weight
- Age, height, activity level, or fitness goal
- General strength or nutrition standards

Your performance is not compared against personal information collected from other LiftXP users.

These results are estimates and may differ because of technique, equipment, exercise variation, range of motion, fatigue, body composition, or inaccurate information.

## 18. Fitness, Nutrition, BodyScan, and AI Disclaimer

LiftXP is a fitness and nutrition tracking tool. It is not a healthcare provider, medical device, diagnostic service, or substitute for professional advice.

LiftXP does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Workout suggestions, strength rankings, nutrition targets, food estimates, AI-generated workouts, and other results may be incomplete, inaccurate, or unsuitable for your circumstances.

BodyScan is provided for entertainment and self-tracking. It produces subjective visual fitness-presentation scores from a photograph. It does not measure body-fat percentage, actual muscle mass, health, fitness capacity, genetics, anatomy, or medical condition.

“Muscle Mass” and every other BodyScan result are visual scores, not clinical measurements. BodyScan scores may change substantially because of lighting, pose, clothing, camera angle, framing, image quality, and AI uncertainty.

Review AI-generated workouts before performing them and verify meal estimates before saving or relying on them.

Consult a qualified healthcare, fitness, or nutrition professional before making significant changes to exercise or diet, particularly if you have an injury, medical condition, dietary restriction, or other health concern.

Stop exercising and seek appropriate medical attention if you experience pain, dizziness, difficulty breathing, or other concerning symptoms.
